  • TOEFL

    TOEFL: Test of English as a Foreign Language is one of the two major English-language tests (the other being the IELTS) accepted by more than 11,000 universities and institutions in over 190 countries and territories. There are three types of TOEFL exam: The TOEFL internet-based test (iBT), Computer-based test (CBT), and Paper-based test (PBT)

    All the three tests measure all four academic English skills- reading, writing, listening, and speaking; where one of the sections will include extra, uncounted material.

  • PTE

    PTE or Pearson Test of English is a computer-based English language test conducted for non-native speakers who wish to study abroad. This academic exam is accepted by thousands of educational institutions across the world. Moreover, it is also accepted by the government of Australia and New Zealand for visa purposes.

    PTE test is designed in such a way that every question generally tests two skills simultaneously, like reading and speaking, listening and writing. The two-hour-long computer based test focuses on day-to-day English rather than high-level English. Unlike many other English proficiency tests, PTE results are declared typically in two business days; hence it is the first choice of the students who are in a bit of rush.

How long it takes to Learn English Language

Learning English can be challenging, But most of people know and understand English. No matter what your first language is, and even if English appears easy at first and difficult to master, don't give up.

If you're wondering if there are any shortcuts or specialised strategies to learn English, We've listed a couple in this tutorial that will assist you in making faster progress. There is no set amount of time that can guarantee your success in learning English, but persistence is the most crucial factor. You're not going to wake up one day and be able to communicate fluently in English.

That's something you'd only see in a movie. You'll see results quickly if you just take one step at a time. Learning a new language takes a varying amount of time depending on a variety of factors such as prior experience and exposure to the language, resilience, effort put into the learning process, motivation, and so on. However, if you practise on a regular basis for at least three to six months, you should be able to hold a daily conversation with a buddy and do tasks such as entering a cafe and ordering in English. Some people struggle more than others and take longer to reach that level, but this is usually due to a lack of effort and daily practise. It will certainly take a few years of practise to be able to speak English fluently, but we're only excited to get started.
